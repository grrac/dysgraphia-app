Severity grades

How each classification level is defined and what action it suggests.

Low Risk

Handwriting is largely legible with minimal irregularities. Patterns fall within typical developmental ranges.

No immediate intervention required. Routine monitoring is sufficient.
Moderate Risk

Noticeable irregularities in letter formation, spacing, or legibility. Some patterns align with dysgraphia markers.

Professional screening is recommended. Early support strategies may be beneficial.
High Risk

Multiple consistent markers of dysgraphia present. Significant impact on legibility and fluency observed.

Referral to an educational psychologist or occupational therapist is strongly advised.

About the model: Trained on handwriting from 60 respondents across three severity levels. Deep image features (ResNet-18) are classified by a support-vector model into Low, Moderate, or High risk. High-Risk recall (56.7%) is the known limitation — roughly half of severe cases are not flagged — so the tool is a first-pass screener, not a diagnostic.
